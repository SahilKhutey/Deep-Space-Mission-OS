# Mathematical Knowledge System

The mathematical foundation for:
- Deep Space Mission Planner
- Deep Space Propulsion Simulator
- Deep Space Spacecraft Digital Twin

## Architecture

Pure Mathematics
        ↓
Applied Mathematics
        ↓
Physics Equations
        ↓
Engineering Models
        ↓
Numerical Simulations
        ↓
Mission Analysis
        ↓
Digital Twin

## 12 Mathematical Domains

| # | Domain | Foundation For |
|---|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Algebra | Rocket equation, mass models, constraints |
| 2 | Geometry & Trigonometry | Orbits, spacecraft, visualization, attitude |
| 3 | Calculus | Motion, fuel, energy, optimization |
| 4 | Differential Equations | Orbit propagation, control |
| 5 | Linear Algebra | Transformations, estimation, ML |
| 6 | Numerical Methods | Solvers, propagators, interpolation |
| 7 | Probability & Statistics | Risk, Monte Carlo, Bayesian |
| 8 | Optimization | Trajectory design, fuel minimization |
| 9 | Dynamical Systems | Stability, Lagrange points, chaos |
| 10 | Astrodynamics Math | Kepler, Lambert, orbital energy |
| 11 | Control Theory | GNC, state space, observability |
| 12 | ML Mathematics | AI mission engineer |

## Implementation Rule

Every equation:
1. Has a docstring with source and physics background
2. Has reference validation tests
3. Is dimensionally consistent
4. Links to the software module that uses it
